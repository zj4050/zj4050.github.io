Spectroscopic Analysis of OH-Megamaser IRAS 11506-3851

Introduction

OH-Megamaser galaxies are the galaxies emitting the hydroxl maser with the infrared luminosity \(L_\text{IR} \ge 10^{12} L_\odot\). Many studies have shown that these galaxies can host AGNs at their center.

However, these galaxies have dense layers of dust and gas that could block out the AGN radiation. We can analyse the ionziation behaviors of the nearby environments to see whether there is an AGN at center or not.

Overview

I only did this project for a semester-long.
During this project, I implemented the code PPXF to fit the spectra of IRAS 11506-3851. I have also plotted BPT diagram to show the line ratio to see whether this galaxy hosts an AGN or not.

Based on the BPT diagram, this galaxy seems to be a starburst galaxy without an AGN at the center.
